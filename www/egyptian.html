Fundamental Thinking Models
⚡
Core Concept Triggers
Mental anchors for rapid reasoning
◆Force → changes motion, not required for motion.
◆Energy → bookkeeping tool, always conserved or converted.
◆Field → action without contact.
◆Potential → "stored effect", drives flow.
◆Symmetry → simplifies problems, implies equilibrium or extremum.
◆Limiting cases → check correctness, understand extreme behaviour.
◆Dimensions → verify equations, eliminate options.
◆Graph slope/area → rate of change / accumulation.
◆Equilibrium → net force and net torque are both zero.
◆Conservation Laws → apply when external forces/torques are absent.
◆Wave → energy transfer, no matter transfer.
◆Superposition → net effect = sum of individual effects.
🔭
Motion & Field Triggers
Projectile, SHM, EM fields at a glance
◆Projectile → horizontal velocity constant; vertical under gravity.
◆Circular motion → acceleration towards center; velocity tangent.
◆SHM → restoring force ∝ displacement; energy oscillates KE ↔ PE.
◆Capacitor → stores energy (electric field), blocks DC, passes AC.
◆Inductor → stores energy (magnetic field), opposes current change.
◆Magnetic force → perpendicular to velocity and field, does no work.
◆Electric field lines → originate +ve, terminate −ve, never cross.
◆Magnetic field lines → form closed loops, never cross.
◆p-n forward bias → potential barrier ↓, current flows.
◆p-n reverse bias → potential barrier ↑, no current.
❓
Question Pattern Recognition
Strategic entry points for exam questions
◆"What happens if…" → use proportionality, identify direct/inverse relations.
◆Graph change → slope/area logic, identify turning points.
◆Comparison → use ratios, not absolute values (e.g. F₁/F₂ = (r₂/r₁)²).
◆Extremum → occurs at symmetry, 45°, center, or by differentiation.
◆Elimination → check units, dimensions, limiting cases, options.
◆"Which is correct?" → test each option with a conceptual check.
◆"Statement 1 & 2" → check independence and logical connection.
◆Multiple forces → resolve into components, apply Newton's laws.
◆Energy conservation → apply when non-conservative forces do no work.
◆Momentum conservation → apply when external impulse is zero.

Relations & Proportionality
Key direct and inverse dependencies
◆Frequency ↑ → wavelength ↓ (constant speed)
◆Radius ↑ → gravitational force ↓ (inverse square)
◆Temperature ↑ → R ↑ metals; R ↓ semiconductors
◆Current ↑ → magnetic field strength ↑
◆Charge ↑ → electric field strength ↑
◆Distance ↑ → intensity ↓ (inverse square, point source)
◆Mass ↑ → inertia ↑
◆Moment of inertia ↑ → angular acceleration ↓
◆Pendulum length ↑ → time period ↑
◆Spring const ↑ → oscillation frequency ↑

Mechanics — Laws of Motion, Energy & Gravitation
∑
Kinematics & Newton's Laws
Equations of motion and force
◆Displacement: s = ut + ½at²
◆Velocity: v = u + at
◆Third equation: v² = u² + 2as
◆Newton's 2nd Law: F = ma
◆Momentum: p = mv
◆Kinetic Energy: K = ½mv² = p²/2m
◆PE (Gravity): U = mgh
◆PE (Spring): U = ½kx²
◆Work Done: W = F · s · cosθ
◆Power: P = F · v = dW/dt
τ
Rotation & Gravitation
Torque, orbital mechanics, Kepler
◆Torque: τ = r × F = Iα
◆Angular Momentum: L = r × p = Iω
◆Moment of Inertia: I = Σmr²
◆Centripetal Force: Fc = mv²/r = mω²r
◆Angular Frequency: ω = 2πf = 2π/T
◆Gravitational Force: F = GMm/r²
◆Grav. PE: U = −GMm/r
◆Escape Velocity: vₑ = √(2GM/R)
◆Orbital Velocity: vₒ = √(GM/R)
◆Kepler's 3rd: T² ∝ R³
η
Fluid Mechanics & Elasticity
Pressure, Bernoulli, Stokes, Young's
◆Fluid Pressure: P = ρgh
◆Bernoulli: P + ½ρv² + ρgh = const
◆Stokes' Law: Fᵥ = 6πηrv
◆Terminal Velocity: vₜ = 2r²(ρₚ−ρf)g / 9η
◆Young's Modulus: Y = FL / AΔL
◆Surface Tension: T = F/L
◆Capillary Rise: h = 2T cosθ / ρrg
◆SHM (Spring): T = 2π√(m/k)
◆SHM (Pendulum): T = 2π√(L/g)

Thermodynamics, Gases & Wave Physics
ΔU
Thermodynamics
Gas laws, heat engines, entropy
◆Ideal Gas Law: PV = nRT = NkT
◆First Law: ΔU = Q − W
◆Work (isobaric): W = PΔV
◆Work (isothermal): W = nRT ln(V₂/V₁)
◆Isothermal process: PV = constant
◆Adiabatic process: PVᵞ = constant
◆Carnot Efficiency: η = 1 − T_cold/T_hot
◆Specific Heat: Q = mcΔT
◆Latent Heat: Q = mL
Entropy: ΔS ≥ 0 for any isolated system (Second Law).
λ
Wave Physics & Sound
Speed, Doppler, standing waves, beats
◆Wave Speed: v = fλ
◆Speed of Sound (gas): v = √(γP/ρ)
◆Speed of Sound (solid): v = √(Y/ρ)
◆Intensity: I ∝ A²f²
◆Beat Frequency: fᵦ = |f₁ − f₂|
◆Doppler Effect: f' = f(v ± vₒ)/(v ∓ vₛ)
◆String / Open Pipe: fₙ = nv/2L
◆Closed Pipe: fₙ = (2n−1)v/4L
v ∝ √T for sound in gas. Source towards observer → f ↑; source away → f ↓.

Quick Conceptual Anchors
Thermo & wave reasoning triggers
◆Heat engine efficiency < 100% (Carnot limit is a law).
◆Pressure ↑ → boiling point ↑.
◆Speed of sound depends on medium, not source frequency.
◆Interference requires coherent sources.
◆Diffraction requires aperture ≈ wavelength.
◆P–V isothermal: rectangular hyperbola.
◆P–V adiabatic: steeper than isothermal.
◆Power lost in transmission: Joule heating (I²R).

Electrostatics, Current Electricity, Magnetism & EMI
E
Electrostatics & Capacitance
Coulomb, fields, potential, capacitors
◆Coulomb's Law: F = kq₁q₂/r²
◆Electric Field: E = F/q = kq/r²
◆Electric Potential: V = kq/r
◆PE: U = qV
◆Capacitance: C = Q/V
◆Parallel plate C: C = ε₀A/d
◆Energy in C: U = ½CV² = Q²/2C
◆Series: 1/Cₑq = Σ1/Cᵢ → ↓C
◆Parallel: Cₑq = ΣCᵢ → ↑C
I
Current Electricity
Ohm's law, Kirchhoff, power
◆Ohm's Law: V = IR
◆Resistance: R = ρL/A
◆Electrical Power: P = VI = I²R = V²/R
◆KVL: ΣV = 0 around loop
◆KCL: ΣI = 0 at junction
◆Series R: Rₑq = ΣRᵢ → ↑R, same I
◆Parallel R: 1/Rₑq = Σ1/Rᵢ → ↓R, same V
In series: current is same. In parallel: voltage is same.
B
Magnetism & EMI
Biot-Savart, Faraday, inductance, AC
◆Magnetic force: F = q(v × B)
◆Force on wire: F = I(L × B)
◆Ampere's Law: ∮B·dL = μ₀Iₑₙc
◆Solenoid field: B = μ₀nI
◆Faraday's Law: ε = −dΦ_B/dt
◆Motional EMF: ε = Blv
◆Energy in L: U = ½LI²
◆Inductors series: Lₑq = ΣLᵢ
AC
AC Circuits & Transformers
RMS, reactance, impedance, resonance
◆AC RMS: Iᵣₘₛ = I₀/√2, Vᵣₘₛ = V₀/√2
◆Capacitive reactance: Xc = 1/ωC
◆Inductive reactance: X_L = ωL
◆RLC Impedance: Z = √[R² + (X_L−Xc)²]
◆Resonance: f₀ = 1/(2π√LC), Z = R
◆Transformer: Vₛ/Vₚ = Nₛ/Nₚ = Iₚ/Iₛ
◆Q-factor: Q = (1/R)√(L/C)
At resonance: X_L = Xc, Z = R, power factor = 1.

Optics & Modern Physics
n
Ray Optics
Mirrors, lenses, refraction, TIR
◆Mirror formula: 1/f = 1/v + 1/u
◆Lens formula: 1/f = 1/v − 1/u
◆Lens maker's: 1/f = (n−1)(1/R₁ − 1/R₂)
◆Magnification: m = −v/u = hᵢ/h₀
◆Snell's Law: n₁sinθ₁ = n₂sinθ₂
◆Critical angle: sin C = nᵣ/nᵈ
◆Lens power: P = 1/f (dioptres)
◆Two lenses in contact: 1/F = 1/f₁ + 1/f₂
◆Thin prism deviation: δ = (n−1)A
◆Brewster's angle: tan θₚ = n
∿
Wave Optics
YDSE, diffraction, interference, Malus
◆YDSE fringe width: β = λD/d
◆Constructive (path diff): nλ
◆Destructive (path diff): (n + ½)λ
◆Single slit minima: d sinθ = nλ
◆Malus' Law: I = I₀cos²θ
◆Telescope resolution: 1.22λ/D
◆Microscope resolution: 1.22λ/(2n sinθ)
Interference needs coherent sources. Diffraction needs aperture ≈ λ.
hν
Modern Physics I — Photons & Bohr
Photoelectric effect, de Broglie, atomic model
◆Photon energy: E = hf = hc/λ
◆Photon momentum: p = h/λ
◆Photoelectric eqn: Kₘₐₓ = hf − φ₀
◆de Broglie: λ = h/p = h/√(2mK)
◆Bohr angular momentum: L = nℏ = nh/2π
◆H-atom energy: Eₙ = −13.6/n² eV
◆Bohr radius: rₙ = n²a₀, a₀ = 0.529 Å
◆Spectral lines: 1/λ = Rₕ(1/n₁² − 1/n₂²)
◆Heisenberg: ΔxΔp ≥ ħ/2

Nuclear & Semiconductor Physics
Radioactivity, fission/fusion, diodes
◆Mass-energy: E = mc²
◆Decay law: N = N₀e^(−λt)
◆Half-life: T½ = 0.693/λ
◆Activity: A = λN
◆Mean life: τ = 1/λ
◆Q-value: (Mᵣₑₐc − Mₚᵣₒd)c²
◆Nuclear radius: R = R₀A^(1/3)
◆1 amu = 931.5 MeV
◆Max BE/nucleon → Iron (A ≈ 56)

Graph Intuition, Devices & Mental Shortcuts
📈
Graph Interpretation
Slope, area, shape recognition
◆Slope → rate of change (v-t slope = acceleration; P-V slope = dP/dV)
◆Area under curve → accumulation (F-x = work; v-t = displacement)
◆Horizontal line → constant quantity (constant velocity, constant force)
◆Curve flattening → saturation, decreasing rate of change
◆Parabolic graph → quadratic relation
◆Asymptote → quantity approaches a limit
◆V–I (ohmic) → straight line through origin
◆KE vs v → parabola (K ∝ v²)
◆Radioactive decay → exponential decrease
◆SHM (θ vs t) → sinusoidal
◆RC charging current → exponential rise
◆YDSE intensity → cos² pattern
◆Stopping potential vs f → straight line, slope = h/e

Experiments & Devices
Working principles, null methods, applications
◆Meter bridge → Wheatstone principle; unknown R by balancing ratio.
◆Potentiometer → compares EMFs; no current drawn at null point.
◆Galvanometer → to Ammeter: parallel shunt; to Voltmeter: series high R.
◆Cyclotron → magnetic field for circular path; electric field for acceleration.
◆Transformer → mutual induction, AC only, step-up V↑I↓, step-down V↓I↑.
◆Optical fiber → Total Internal Reflection (TIR).
◆Photodiode → reverse biased; converts light to electrical energy.
◆Zener diode → reverse breakdown for voltage regulation.
◆Solar cell → photoelectric effect; converts light to electricity.
◆LED → forward biased; electrical energy → light.
◆Transistor (CE) → Ic = βIB; amplifier or switch.
◆Logic gates → AND (all 1→1), OR (any 1→1), NOT (invert), NAND, NOR.

Quick Mental Shortcuts
Exam-speed reasoning techniques
◆Use ratios instead of full calculation (F ∝ 1/r²).
◆Use symmetry to eliminate options or simplify geometry.
◆Use extreme cases to test answers (r → ∞, m → 0).
◆Check units/dimensions for quick option elimination.
◆Order of magnitude estimation for numerical problems.
◆Common values: g = 10 m/s², c = 3 × 10⁸ m/s.
◆Identify conserved quantities (energy, momentum, angular momentum).
◆For graphs: instantly interpret slope and area.
◆If stuck: think about what changes and what remains constant.
◆Projectile range max at 45°; max height at 90°.

Common Traps, Exceptions & Units
🚨
Classic Conceptual Traps
Errors that cost marks
◆Velocity = 0 ≠ Acceleration = 0 (e.g. peak of projectile).
◆Work done can be zero even if force exists (centripetal force, magnetic force).
◆Current flows due to potential difference, not absolute charge amount.
◆Heavier object does NOT fall faster (in vacuum, all fall same rate).
◆Electric field inside conductor = 0 (electrostatic equilibrium).
◆Magnetic force never changes speed or KE of a charged particle.
◆Real image is inverted; virtual image is erect (usually).
◆Radioactivity is unaffected by pressure, temperature, or chemical state.
◆Half-life is independent of initial amount.
◆Work done by friction is always negative (non-conservative).
◆TIR only when light goes denser → rarer AND angle > critical angle.
◆Photoelectric Kₘₐₓ → depends on frequency; current on intensity.
◆Heisenberg → fundamental limit, NOT due to measurement error.
◆Nuclear density → constant for all nuclei (not proportional to mass number).
⚡
Circuit & Optics Traps
Frequently confused results
◆Capacitors parallel → C increases; series → C decreases.
◆Resistors parallel → R decreases; series → R increases.
◆Real depth > apparent depth (denser to rarer refraction).
◆Virtual image → cannot be formed on screen.
◆Displacement current → Maxwell's correction to Ampere's law (inside capacitor).
◆Magnetic field inside ideal solenoid → uniform.
◆E inside conductor → zero; V inside conductor → constant (both static).
◆Diode forward → ON; reverse bias → OFF (very small leakage).
◆Transistor depends on light doping of emitter, heavy doping of base.
